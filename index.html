Uruguay

"Libertad o Muerte"

Forces lead to revolution

  1. Uruguay's revolt against Spain was initiated in 1811 by José Gervasio Artigas
  2. Artigas is known to Uruguayans as the father of Uruguayan independence
  3. Artigas attempt to gain autonomy for the country was unsuccessful. Worked in 1828
  4. British diplomatic mediation ended the conflict
  5. Civil wars and foreign intervention went on until end of nineteenth century

Daily life/family

  1. Born in Montevideo on June 19, 1764.
  2. At 12, He was a gaucho, or cowboy, until 1810
  3. Artigas had 4 brothers and 1 sister
  4. At 33, Artigas entered to corps to protect the border
  5. Artigas commanded Uruguayan patriots agaisnt Spain
  6. Artigas helped the fight of buenos aires, and later was captured

Factors that lead to revolution

  1. Discrimination against Montevideo in industrial affairs.
  2. Political issue concerned by Artiga
  3. Artiga was going for a system based on unitary centralism.
  4. Portuguese invade the territory and the Artigas fought them off.
  5. Declaration of colonies.
